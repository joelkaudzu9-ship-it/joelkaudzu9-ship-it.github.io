Who I Am

Executive Overview

I operate at the intersection of healthcare, technology, and disciplined execution.

Hi, I'm Joel George Kaudzu. I'm a Premed Dental Surgery student, Innovation Coordinator, Python developer, and medical-tech enthusiast. I've built a track record of turning ideas into tangible solutions — from my early experiments in secondary school to projects I'm leading today at university.

Currently, I serve as the KUHES Science for All Innovations Coordinator, where I identify, organize, and develop student-driven initiatives into impactful programs. I've coordinated 10+ innovation initiatives and mentored 10+ students.

My innovation journey began in 2021 as a Form Two student at Dedza Secondary School, when I built a mini bio-digester — my first hands-on experiment in applied problem-solving. Since then, I've continued to explore, create, and teach. I spent 1.5 years as a student teacher at Bright Future Private Secondary School, teaching Mathematics and Physics.

At university, I founded CheckMySmile, a dental awareness platform developed and tested by fellow medical students. Thousands of avoidable pain and systemic complications could be curbed with early detection. Currently, I'm developing KUHES Campus Connect, an exclusive student networking platform.

My long-term vision is to blend dentistry, immunology, and data science into scalable solutions that can transform healthcare in Africa. I work in the quiet hours — the "3:33am shift" — building skills, knowledge, and systems that compound over time.

I value discipline, depth, and execution above applause. I believe the most meaningful work is done quietly, consistently, and with purpose.
