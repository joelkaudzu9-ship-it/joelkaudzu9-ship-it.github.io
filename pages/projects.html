CheckMySmile

2024 · Launched MedTech · Web App Solo Founder

The Problem

Dental health is often ignored until pain forces attention. Students, especially, neglect oral hygiene due to lack of engaging tools and awareness. In Malawi, dental education is minimal and preventive care is rare.

The Solution

CheckMySmile makes oral health trackable and engaging. Users log daily smiles, receive reminders, access educational content, and can connect with dental professionals. The platform turns a chore into a habit.

Technical Implementation

  • Python Flask backend with SQLite database
  • Responsive HTML/CSS frontend with dark theme
  • User authentication and progress tracking
  • Educational content management system
  • Demo available on YouTube

Impact & Metrics

  • 100+ active student users
  • Partnered with 2 school dental clinics
  • Featured in KUHES innovation showcase
  • 30% increase in dental visit bookings among users

Next Steps

Adding AI-powered smile analysis, expanding to 5 more schools, and developing a mobile app version.

KUHES Campus Connect

2025 · In Progress Networking · Education

The Problem

Students at KUHES operate in silos—medical students rarely interact with engineering students, yet healthcare innovation requires interdisciplinary collaboration. No centralized platform exists for academic networking.

The Solution

Campus Connect uses @kuhes.ac.mw emails to create a verified, exclusive network where students can find collaborators, share resources, and build projects together. Think LinkedIn, but for campus innovation.

Features

  • Email-verified profiles (institutional accounts only)
  • Project matchmaking based on skills and interests
  • Resource sharing: notes, research papers, opportunities
  • Event discovery and collaboration spaces

Tech Stack

  • Django backend with PostgreSQL
  • REST API for future mobile app
  • JWT authentication with email verification
  • Responsive Bootstrap frontend (customized)

Current Status

MVP development at 60%. Beta launch planned for Q3 2025 with 100 pilot users.

Mini Bio-Digester (2021)

2021 · Completed Engineering · Sustainability

The Spark

As a Form 2 student at Dedza Secondary School, I became fascinated by how waste could become energy. With minimal resources, I designed and built a working mini bio-digester that converted organic waste into biogas.

The Build

  • Used PVC pipes, drums, and basic plumbing
  • Designed anaerobic digestion chamber
  • Captured methane for small-scale cooking demonstration
  • Documented整个过程 with hand-drawn diagrams

Lessons Learned

This project taught me that innovation doesn't require expensive labs—it requires curiosity, patience, and the willingness to get your hands dirty. It sparked my lifelong love for building.

Recognition
