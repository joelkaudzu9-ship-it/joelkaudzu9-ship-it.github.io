The Journey So Far

🌍 Roots & Early Curiosity

Born and raised in Malawi, my journey into innovation began not in a lab, but in the red soil of Dedza. As a Form 2 student at Dedza Secondary School, I built my first real project—a mini bio-digester—out of curiosity and a desire to solve real problems. That project taught me that innovation isn't about resources; it's about resourcefulness.

📚 Academic Foundation

Currently pursuing Premed Dental Surgery at KUHES, I've developed a deep appreciation for the human body, especially the nervous system and chromosomal biology. But I quickly realized that medicine alone isn't enough—technology must be part of the healing equation. This realization set me on a path to blend dentistry with data science, AI, and digital health solutions.

👨‍🏫 The Teacher Within

For 1.5 years at Bright Future Private Secondary School, I stood in front of classrooms teaching Mathematics and Physics. Those 18 months shaped me more than any textbook. I learned to break down complex ideas, to read confusion in a student's eyes, and to find joy in someone else's "aha!" moment. Teaching isn't just something I did—it's part of who I am.

💡 Innovation Leadership

As KUHES Science For All Innovations Coordinator, I don't just manage projects—I cultivate innovators. I've coordinated 10+ student initiatives and mentored 10+ students, building systems that take raw ideas and turn them into executable initiatives.

🚀 Founder Mode

CheckMySmile was born from a simple observation: dental health is neglected until it hurts. I built a platform that makes oral health engaging and trackable, developed and tested by fellow medical students. KUHES Campus Connect is next—an exclusive network for students to collaborate across disciplines.

📊 Data Science Journey

I'm currently deep in a structured transformation into data analytics. Python, pandas, visualization, statistics—I'm not just learning, I'm building. Every new skill is immediately applied to healthcare problems.

📝 The Poet's Mind

I write poetry through my collection "Threads of Becoming." Poetry sharpens my emotional intelligence—essential for a future healer. My 20-page handwritten journal is evidence that I value introspection as much as execution.

⏰ The 3:33am Philosophy

While others sleep, I build. Not out of hustle culture obsession, but because silence breeds clarity. At 3:33am, there are no distractions—just me, the vision, and the work. This discipline compounds.

🌅 Long-Term Vision

I see a future where African healthcare is transformed by its own innovators—solutions built here, for here. I want to be part of that wave, blending dental science with immunology data, building diagnostic tools accessible in rural clinics, and proving that world-class innovation can emerge from Malawi.
