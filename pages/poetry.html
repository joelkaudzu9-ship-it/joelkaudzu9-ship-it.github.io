The Poet Behind the Scientist

Where precision meets feeling · The emotional intelligence behind the innovation

Before I learned anatomy, I learned to feel. Before I understood the nervous system, I understood longing. Poetry isn't separate from my science — it's the soul of it. These verses are windows into the inner world that drives the outer work.

Selected Works

I

Scalpel & Verse

The scalpel knows what the heart forgets —
every incision is a question,
every suture, a prayer for wholeness.

I cut to heal,
I write to feel,
and in the space between
bone and metaphor,
I find myself.

The body is a poem
we spend our lives learning to read.

II

3:33am

The world sleeps,
but I am awake
with my thoughts and my code.

Darkness outside,
light inside —
the monitor glows
like a confession.

This is the hour of builders,
of dreamers who don't dream
but build their dreams
keystroke by keystroke.

When the sun rises,
they'll ask how.
I'll point to the dark.

III

Healing

Healing is not fixing —
it's reminding the body
of the wholeness it forgot.

The physician is not a mechanic
but a gardener,
creating conditions
for the innate to emerge.

I learned this not in textbooks
but in silence,
watching wounds close
and hearts mend.

The body knows the way.
We just help it remember.

2023
IV

Anatomy Lesson

I learned anatomy in the dark,
tracing bones like constellations —
the body, a universe
waiting to be understood.

The femur, a river;
the skull, a dome;
the heart, a fist
that opens and closes
with the rhythm of wanting.

Every cadaver was once someone,
every scar a story,
every organ a poem
I'm still learning to read.

2024
V

The Waiting

In the waiting, I learned
the anatomy of hope —
how it expands and contracts
with each breath.

Patients wait for news,
students wait for results,
builders wait for deployment —
and in the waiting,
we become who we are.

Patience is not passive.
It is the most active form of trust.

2024
VI

Legacy

I want my legacy to be
not monuments, but minds —
students who think,
builders who build,
healers who heal.

A tree is known by its fruit.
A life, by the lives it touches.
I want to touch without grasping,
teach without possessing,
build without owning.

When I'm gone,
let them say not what I made,
but who I helped become.

2025

Why Poetry Matters in Medicine

Medicine without poetry is technique without soul. The best physicians I've studied — Osler, Gawande, Verghese — all understood that healing requires more than algorithms. It requires empathy, imagination, and the ability to see the person behind the patient.

Poetry trains these muscles. It teaches me to sit with ambiguity, to feel without fixing, to witness without judging. Every poem is a patient encounter: a story waiting to be heard, a truth waiting to emerge.

When I write, I practice the same skills I'll need at the bedside: attention, presence, and the courage to sit with suffering without running away.
